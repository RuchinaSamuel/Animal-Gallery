AMPHIBIANS

Name:AFRICAN BULLFROG
Scientific Name: Pyxicephalus adspersus
Where found: Africa
The African bullfrog is a large frog found in southern Africa. It lives in a variety of habitats, including savannas, shrublands, marshes and farmland. The species is a voracious predator; its diet includes a variety of species, including insects, small mammals, reptiles and other amphibians including African bullfrog tadpoles! Although the African bullfrog has sharp teeth and will bite if it feels threatened, the species is kept as an exotic pet.
Name: African Common Toad / Square-Marked Toad
Scientific Name: Amietophrynus regularis
Where found: Africa
The African common toad goes by a number of other names, including: square-marked toad, African toad, Egyptian toad, African bouncing toad and Reuss’s toad. The species is found throughout sub-Saharan Africa (the area south of the Sahara desert), and lives in a number of different habitats. This large, warty-skinned African amphibian grows to lengths of up to 13 cm / 5.12 in.
Name: Fire Salamander
Scientific Name: Salamandra salamandra
Where found: Throughout Europe
Salamanders are a group of amphibians typically characterized by their lizard-like appearance, with slender bodies, blunt snouts, short limbs projecting at right angles to the body, and the presence of a tail in both larvae and adults. All ten extant salamander families are grouped together under the order Urodela from the group Caudata.[2] Salamander diversity is highest in eastern North America, especially in the Appalachian Mountains; most species are found in the Holarctic realm, with some species present in the Neotropical realm.

FISHES

Name: Asian arowana
Scientific Name: Scleropages formosus
Where found: Southeast Asia
There are several recognized species of Asian Arowanas, including the Green Arowana (Scleropages formosus), Red Arowana (Scleropages macrocephalus), and Silver Arowana (Scleropages jardinii), among others. In many Asian cultures, particularly in countries like China, the Asian Arowana is considered a symbol of luck, prosperity, and wealth. It is often referred to as the "dragon fish" due to its resemblance to traditional Chinese dragon imagery.
Name: Arctic char
Scientific Name: Salvelinus alpinus
Where found: Eurasia, Europe, North-America
Arctic char have a distinct appearance, often displaying vibrant colors that range from pale yellow to bright red, orange, and even dark green. Their coloration can change based on their environment and life stage. They have a streamlined body with small, pale spots on their back, sides, and fins. Arctic char are well-adapted to cold environments and are typically found in freshwater lakes, rivers, and coastal waters of the Arctic and subarctic regions. They are known to inhabit both freshwater and marine environments, making them a unique species among salmonids.
Name: Trout
Scientific Name: Oncorhynchus mykiss
Where found: North America, northern Asia and Europe
Trout is a generic common name for numerous species of carnivorous freshwater ray-finned fishes belonging to the genera Oncorhynchus, Salmo and Salvelinus, all of which are members of the subfamily Salmoninae in the family Salmonidae. Trout are carnivorous and feed on a variety of aquatic insects, crustaceans, smaller fish, and even terrestrial insects that fall into the water.

MAMMALS

Name: Zonkey
Scientific Name: Equus zebra x Equus asinus
Where found: Africa
A “zonkey” is a hybrid cross produced by mating a zebra and a donkey. Zonkeys are not a true species because they have an odd number of chromosomes and cannot reproduce. While zonkeys are rare, they are bred in a number of zoos and specialized farms around the world. Zonkeys are also referred to as zebonkey, zebrinny, zebrula, zebadonks, and several other interesting names, depending on who is breeding them and what combination of donkey and zebra species are being used. Typically, zonkeys are very rare in nature and are usually only seen in zoos or on game-animal farms.
Name: Yorkshire Terrier
Scientific Name: Canis lupus
Where found: Europe
The Yorkshire Terrier is a British breed of toy dog of terrier type. It is among the smallest of the terriers and indeed of all dog breeds, with a weight of no more than 3.2 kg/7 pounds. It originated in the nineteenth century in the English county of Yorkshire, for which it is named. The Yorkshire Terrier, often referred to as a "Yorkie," is a small dog breed known for its charming appearance and lively personality.Yorkshire Terriers originated in the county of Yorkshire, England, during the 19th century. They were originally bred to catch rats and mice in clothing mills and mines.
Name: Zorse
Scientific Name: Equus zebra x Equus caballus
Where found: Africa, North-America
A zorse is a hybrid animal that results from the crossbreeding of a zebra and a horse. The term "zorse" is a portmanteau of "zebra" and "horse." This crossbreeding typically involves a male zebra and a female horse, as the physical and genetic differences between the two species can make it challenging for a female zebra to carry a hybrid pregnancy to term. Zorses can exhibit a wide range of physical characteristics, depending on the specific zebra and horse breeds involved in the cross. They often have stripes reminiscent of a zebra's pattern, usually on their legs, neck, or body. The base coat color can vary, and they might inherit the coloration from either parent.

BIRDS

Name: Hummingbirds
Scientific Name: Trochilidae
Where found: Americas, from Alaska to Tierra del Fuego
Hummingbirds are fascinating and unique birds known for their small size, vibrant plumage, and rapid wing movements. Hummingbirds are known for their ability to hover in mid-air by rapidly flapping their wings. They primarily feed on nectar from flowers using their specialized long, slender bills and extendable, tube-like tongues. Nectar is their main source of energy, but they also consume insects and spiders for protein.
Name: Parrots
Scientific Name: Psittacidae
Where found: South America, Australia, Africa, and Southeast Asia.
Parrots are colorful and intelligent birds that belong to the family Psittacidae. They are known for their vibrant plumage, distinctive vocalizations, and ability to mimic human speech and other sounds. Parrots have strong beaks, zygodactyl feet (with two toes facing forward and two facing backward), and a curved upper beak. These adaptations help them grasp objects and manipulate their environment.Parrots are considered some of the most intelligent birds and are known for problem-solving abilities, tool usage, and complex social behaviors.
Name: Robins
Scientific Name: Turdidae
Where found: forests, woodlands, gardens, parks, and urban areas
Robins are small to medium-sized birds that belong to the Turdidae family. They are known for their distinctive red or orange-red breast and are a common sight in gardens and woodlands in many parts of the world. Robins are known for their melodious songs, which they use for territory establishment and attracting mates. They are also territorial birds, defending their feeding and nesting territories aggressively. Robins are omnivores, feeding on insects, worms, fruits, and berries.

INSECTS

Name: Ants
Scientific Name: Formicidae
Where found: Most species live in soil, leaf litter, or decaying plants
Ants are small social insects that belong to the family Formicidae. They are known for their highly organized colonies and division of labor. Ants are found all around the world and play important roles in ecosystems, including scavenging, seed dispersal, and pest control.
Name: Grasshoppers
Scientific Name: Orthoptera
Where found: Grasslands, Meadows, Forests, Agricultural areas, Urban and suburban areas
Grasshoppers are insects that belong to the suborder Caelifera, which is a part of the order Orthoptera. They are well-known for their ability to jump long distances, owing to their powerful hind legs. Grasshoppers are herbivorous creatures that primarily feed on plants and are found in various habitats, including grasslands, meadows, forests, and even urban areas. Grasshoppers typically have a long and slender body with two pairs of wings. The front wings, known as tegmina, are often hardened and protective, while the hind wings are membranous and used for flying. They also have large compound eyes and powerful mandibles for chewing plant material.Many grasshopper species have evolved to blend into their surroundings through coloration and body shape, which helps them avoid predators.
Name: Beetles
Scientific Name: Coleoptera
Where found: Forests, Grasslands, Deserts, Wetlands, Urban Areas, Caves, Water Bodies, Deadwood and Decomposing Matter.
Beetles are a diverse group of insects belonging to the order Coleoptera, which is the largest order of living organisms on Earth, comprising about 400,000 described species. They can be found in a wide range of habitats, including forests, grasslands, wetlands, and even urban environments. Beetles come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors. Beetles typically have a hard exoskeleton that provides protection and support. Their front pair of wings, called elytra, are usually hard and serve as protective covers for the hind wings and abdomen.

REPTILES

Name: Snakes(Green Anaconda (Eunectes murinus))
Scientific Name: Serpentes
Where found: South America
Anacondas are known for their large size, powerful constriction abilities, and semi-aquatic lifestyle. They are skilled swimmers and are often found in and around water sources such as rivers, swamps, and marshes. These snakes play an important role in their ecosystems as top predators, helping to control prey populations. They primarily feed on a variety of prey, including birds, mammals, and aquatic creatures. Eunectes is a genus of boas found in tropical South America commonly called anacondas. They are a semiaquatic group of snakes and include one of the largest snakes in the world
Name: Geckos
Scientific Name: Gekkonidae
Where found: Geckos can be found in various habitats around the world, ranging from tropical rainforests to deserts. They are most diverse and abundant in warm and tropical regions.
Geckos are a type of lizard that belong to the family Gekkonidae. They are known for their unique features and behaviors, which include the ability to climb vertical surfaces and even walk upside down on ceilings due to specialized toe pads that allow them to adhere to surfaces. One of the most remarkable adaptations of geckos is their ability to vocalize. Some species are known to make distinctive chirping or clicking sounds, which are used for communication and courtship.
Name: Chameleons
Scientific Name: Chamaeleonidae
Where found: Chameleons are found in various regions, primarily in Africa, Madagascar, southern Europe, and some parts of Asia.
Chameleons are a fascinating group of lizards known for their remarkable ability to change color and their unique physical features. One of the most distinctive traits of chameleons is their color-changing ability. They achieve this through a combination of structural and pigment cells in their skin. The color changes can signal various emotions, temperature adjustments, and even communication with other chameleons.
